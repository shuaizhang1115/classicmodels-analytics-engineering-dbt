# Modeling decisions

## Grain

The central analytical grain is **one row per order line**. This grain is used in both `fact_orders` and `orders_obt`.

Why this grain matters:

- Revenue and margin are calculated at the order-line level.
- Product-line and customer analysis require product and customer attributes on each line.
- Composite uniqueness is enforced by `order_number + order_line_number`.

## Surrogate keys

The star schema uses surrogate keys generated by `dbt_utils.generate_surrogate_key` for customer, employee, office, product, and order-line facts.

This pattern decouples analytical joins from operational natural keys and aligns with dimensional modeling best practices.

## Star schema vs OBT

| Design | Strength | Trade-off |
|---|---|---|
| Star schema | Reusable dimensions, cleaner governance, less duplication | Requires joins for analysis |
| OBT | Simple for dashboards and ad hoc queries | Duplicates dimension attributes |

## Data quality strategy

The project applies dbt tests at three levels:

1. **Raw source assumptions**: source primary keys and accepted status values.
2. **Staging integrity**: type-cleaned keys and source relationships.
3. **Mart-level trust**: fact/dimension relationships, primary-key uniqueness, positive measures, and OBT composite uniqueness.
